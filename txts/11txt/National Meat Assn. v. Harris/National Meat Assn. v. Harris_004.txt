JUSTICE KAGAN delivered the opinion of the Court.
The Federal Meat Inspection Act (FMIA or Act), 21
U. S. C. §601 et seq., regulates the inspection, handling,
and slaughter of livestock for human consumption. We
consider here whether the FMIA expressly preempts a
California law dictating what slaughterhouses must do
with pigs that cannot walk, known in the trade as nonambulatory pigs. We hold that the FMIA forecloses the challenged applications of the state statute.
