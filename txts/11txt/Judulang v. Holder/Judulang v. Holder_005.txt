JOEL JUDULANG, PETITIONER v. ERIC H. HOLDER,
JR., ATTORNEY GENERAL
ON WRIT OF CERTIORARI TO THE UNITED STATES COURT OF
APPEALS FOR THE NINTH CIRCUIT
[December 12, 2011]

JUSTICE KAGAN delivered the opinion of the Court.
This case concerns the Board of Immigration Appeals’
(BIA or Board) policy for deciding when resident aliens
may apply to the Attorney General for relief from deportation under a now-repealed provision of the immigration
laws. We hold that the BIA’s approach is arbitrary and
capricious.
The legal background of this case is complex, but the
principle guiding our decision is anything but. When an
administrative agency sets policy, it must provide a reasoned explanation for its action. That is not a high bar,
but it is an unwavering one. Here, the BIA has failed to
meet it.
